## Abstract

This research project presents a data-driven analysis of customer purchase behavior in e-commerce platforms. The study examines how consumer actions such as browsing behavior, inter-purchase timing, product ratings, discount sensitivity, cart abandonment, and device usage influence online purchasing decisions.

Using advanced data analytics techniques, the research identifies meaningful behavioral patterns and customer segments that help businesses improve acquisition, retention, personalization, and operational strategies.

---

## Problem Statement

Understanding customer purchase behavior in e-commerce is essential for improving sales conversion, customer retention, and personalized shopping experiences. However, customer decision-making is influenced by multiple dynamic factors including pricing strategies, reviews, device type, and shopping session behavior.

This research aims to analyze these factors systematically and derive actionable insights for e-commerce platforms.

## Key Findings

### 1. Product Ratings Impact
- Products rated between **4.2–4.7 stars** showed the highest conversion.
- Perfect 5-star ratings performed slightly lower due to possible trust concerns.

### 2. Discount Analysis
- Optimal discount range: **20%–35%**
- Discounts above 50% increased clicks but reduced long-term customer value.

### 3. Customer Segmentation
Four major customer segments were identified:
- Engaged Advocates
- Value Reliables
- Emerging Prospects
- Lapsing Users

### 4. Seasonal Trends
Two major shopping peaks observed:
- Mid-year sales (June)
- Festive shopping season (November–December)

### 5. Cart Abandonment
- Lowest abandonment observed in sessions lasting **6–18 minutes**
- Mobile checkout showed significantly higher abandonment.

### 6. Mobile vs Desktop
- Mobile traffic: 58%
- Mobile transactions: 34%
- Indicates strong checkout optimization opportunities.
